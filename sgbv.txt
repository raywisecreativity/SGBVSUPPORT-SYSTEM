SEXUAL AND GENDER-BASED VIOLENCE (SGBV)

Definition:
Sexual and Gender-Based Violence (SGBV) refers to any harmful act that is perpetrated against a person’s will and is based on socially ascribed gender differences. It includes physical, sexual, emotional, psychological, and economic abuse.

Types of SGBV:
- Physical violence (beating, assault)
- Sexual violence (rape, sexual assault, harassment)
- Emotional and psychological abuse (threats, intimidation, humiliation)
- Economic abuse (denial of financial resources or control over money)
- Harmful traditional practices (forced marriage, female genital mutilation)

Key Facts:
- SGBV can affect anyone regardless of age, gender, or background
- It is never the survivor’s fault
- Survivors have the right to safety, dignity, and care
- Early response (within 72 hours) is very important for medical care

Support and Response:
- Survivors are encouraged to seek immediate help at the nearest Primary Health Centre (PHC)
- Medical services are confidential and can be accessed without a police report
- Emotional support from trusted individuals or professionals is important
- Legal and social support services may also be available

Safety Guidelines:
- Always prioritize your safety
- If you are in immediate danger, seek help from emergency services or nearby authorities
- Reach out to trusted friends, family, or community support systems

Role of This Chatbot:
This chatbot is designed to:
- Provide general information about SGBV
- Offer supportive and non-judgmental responses
- Guide users to appropriate help and services such as PHCs
- Encourage safe and informed decision-making

Important Notice:
This chatbot does NOT replace professional medical, legal, or psychological services. It is only for informational and support purposes.

Always seek help from qualified professionals when needed.
